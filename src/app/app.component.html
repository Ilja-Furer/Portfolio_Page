Mountainbiking

I was able to borrow a bike from a friend to give it a try. Even though that one was old, heavy, and way too small, I immediately fell in love with the sport. Now I've been riding bikes for over six years, made new friends because of it, and convinced old friends to give it a try. I started out with a cross-country bike from Giant but quickly realised that I much prefer gravity riding. So, I sold my Giant and bought two new bikes: an Enduro and a Downhill bike. I started riding almost daily and progressing fast. These days I don’t have that much free time anymore, but I still try to get out as often as possible.
The picture here is our friend group's first time riding in Champéry.

Shortly after I started riding, I discovered the professional Downhill scene. Governed by the Union Cycliste Internationale (International Cycling Union), the best riders compete in 10 races across the globe to determine the fastest. Additionally, the top riders from each country are selected for the World Championship—a one-race, winner-takes-all competition.

This year, Switzerland will host two events: a World Series round in Lenzerheide and the World Championships in Valais. The legendary Downhill track in Champéry will return to the spotlight after a 15-year hiatus, welcoming the world's best riders. I am immensely looking forward to both events.

Swimming

Having lived in multiple different locations, one thing that keeps pulling me back to Bern is the Aare. The Aare snakes down from the Bernese Highlands to Bern and finally ends in lake Bienne. In a way connecting where I was born to where I choose to live now. If I'm not busy or on my bike you'll often find me somewhere close to the riverbed enjoying nature, grilling or getting ready to take a refreshing plunge.
